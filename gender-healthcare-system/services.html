Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our healthcare services

We accept most major insurance plans, including Medicare and Medicaid. Our billing department can work with your insurance provider to determine coverage for specific services. For services not covered by insurance, we offer payment plans and sliding scale fees based on financial need.

Starting hormone therapy typically begins with an initial consultation with one of our healthcare providers specialized in gender-affirming care. This appointment includes a health assessment, discussion of your goals, and education about treatment options. We follow an informed consent model, ensuring you understand all aspects of treatment before proceeding. After baseline testing, a personalized treatment plan will be developed for you.

No, we accept patients without referrals. You can schedule an appointment directly with our clinic. However, some insurance plans may require referrals for certain specialized services to be covered. We recommend checking with your insurance provider regarding their referral requirements before your appointment.

Yes, our healthcare providers can provide assessment and recommendation letters for gender-affirming surgeries following the WPATH Standards of Care guidelines. The process typically involves ongoing care with our providers to establish a therapeutic relationship. Each surgical procedure may have different requirements for documentation, which we can discuss during your consultation.

Yes, we offer telehealth appointments for many of our services, including consultations, follow-ups, and mental health support. Some services will require in-person visits, particularly those involving physical examinations or laboratory testing. Our staff can help determine which appointments can be conducted remotely.
